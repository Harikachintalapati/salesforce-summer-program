Data quality problems: Data quality problems include missing, incorrect, outdated, or inconsistent information in a system. Poor data quality can lead to inaccurate reports and bad business decisions.
Migration discussion: Data migration is the process of transferring data from one system to another while maintaining accuracy and integrity. Proper planning and testing are important to avoid data loss during migration.
Duplicate prevention ideas: Duplicate records can be prevented using validation rules, unique identifiers, and duplicate detection tools. Regular data cleaning and standardized entry processes also improve data accuracy.
Enterprise risks of bad data and reflection: Bad data can cause financial losses, customer dissatisfaction, reporting errors, and operational inefficiencies in enterprises.
Reflection helps organizations analyze data issues and improve data management practices for the future.
